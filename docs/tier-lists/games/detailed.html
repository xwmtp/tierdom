Tiers indicate what games mean to me. Rating is the actual quality of a game (given its context and time period).

S

Zelda: Ocarina of Time

99/100, n64

An absolute classic, a blueprint for numerous games coming after it. There's not a single game that had more impact on my life; I've replayed it numerous times while growing up, it taught me English, and I even met my partner through the OoT speedrunning community. I just give its successor a slightly higher score for being more original.

Zelda: Majora's Mask

100/100, n64

Love everything about this. The storytelling is eerie and unique. Then there is the innovative with the 72-hour loop system, a very bold move by the devs that had the impossible task of making a sequal to OoT. It turned out perfect. The soundtrack includes my all-time favorites. The image of the moon haunts me to this day.

Lylat Wars

95/100, n64

Watched in awe while my parents played this when I was very young, and got into it myself when it came out on the Virtual Console of the Wii. I could not put it down. I've done endless attempts to improve my high score and collect all the medals. Many of the quotes still live rent-free in my head. Do a barrel roll!

Banjo Kazooie

97/100, n64

In my opinion, this was the platformer highlight of the N64, not SM64. It's charming, funny, quirky, and possibly has the coolest overworld I know. You need to collect quite a significant percentage of the available collectibles to progress, which makes the game the right amount of challenging. And have I mentioned Grant Kirkhope's perfect soundtrack???

A

Diddy Kong Racing

90/100, n64

Probably my favorite kart game of all time. It's just very charming. The music and sound effects are so quirky and catchy, and I loved the overworld which you usually don't have in these kind of games. Great memories.

Yoshi's Story

90/100, n64

Cute but difficult game. I love yoshi, and this one might be my favorite. It has an original concept where you play through the whole story in (usually) one sitting, and pick your levels. The better you do, the more choice you have. Hearing the yoshi choir sing still brings a tear to my eye.

77/100, n64

Almost exclusively played the party games, usually with my friends from school. These mini games were way more fun than you'd expect from such a game.

Pokémon Snap

83/100, n64

The concept of this game is original, and a lot more fun than it sounds. I enjoyed redoing the levels over and over to find secrets and improve my photo scores. The gameplay is a bit simple, but the game is short enough to not start to drag.

Wave Race

78/100, n64

Fun and quite challenging jetski race game. The graphics were stunning at the time and the voice of the announcer is quite iconic. The controls are difficult to master, especially on the wavy tracks, but it feels rewarding when you manage to.

Paper Mario

85/100, n64

Probably my first RPG style game! Don't remember all the details, but I have a few vivid memories of a desert world and a ghostly area, among morre. Good game with a cute artstyle.

Pilotwings 64

Had very fond memories of this game, and replayed it all recently when I was ill. Surprisingly, this old game lived up to what I remembered of it! The controls are simple, but the tasks are challenging. It's a very relaxing game, which is partly due to it's great soundtrack.

Snowboard Kids

Quite a strange looking snowboard game looking back now. I've spent countless of after-school afternoons trying to unlock everything there was in this game, and I might be tiering it through nostalgia glasses.

Where's the rest!?

Mmmhhh... these are all the games I remember playing, and for long enough to form an honest opinion on them. There might be a handful missing (especially from longer ago), and I will add them when I think of them. But 90%+ of all games I've ever (seriously) played should be on here.

How stable is this tier list?

Extremely stable! Let me explain.

The tier a game belongs to is how 'impactful' it was to me, how much I enjoyed it given its time and context. There might then also be several cases where a high tier does not automatically imply a recommendation.

New games added (as I play them) will be inserted into the list. But this will leave the list otherwise stable.

What about the xx/100 ratings?

These indicate how high I rate the quality of the game (also given its time period). There's a strong correlation between high tiers and high ratings.

Notable exceptions include for example games that mean a lot to me, but might still have a rather low rating because they're still bad games. Vice versa some games with high quality didn't click for me, so they might end up in a low tier.
