Tiers indicate what games mean to me. Rating is the actual quality of a game (given its context and time period).

S

Zelda: Majora's Mask

100/100, n64

Love everything about this. The storytelling is eerie and unique. Then there is the innovative 72-hour loop system, a very bold move by the devs that had the impossible task of making a sequal to OoT. It turned out perfect. The soundtrack includes my all-time favorites. The image of the moon haunts me to this day.

Zelda: Ocarina of Time

99/100, n64

An absolute classic, a blueprint for numerous games coming after it. There's not a single game that had more impact on my life; I've replayed it numerous times while growing up, it taught me English, and I even met my partner through the OoT speedrunning community. I just give its successor a slightly higher score for being more original.

Outer Wilds

98/100, pc

No other game has ever made me feel the way this game did. I went from euphoria to absolute terror, from tranquil peace to deep curiosity. Whenever I had to put the game down, I could not stop thinking about it until it was time to continue. If you're remotely interested in this game, please don't look up anything and just experience it blank. There is nothing like it.

Celeste

98/100, switch

I'm generally not a huge platformer fan, but this game is so insanely good and I loved every moment of it. At points it was so challenging it almost drove me crazy, but I never lost motivation. I even collected all the strawberries! The soundtrack is one of my favorites of all time, and the story hit home.

Super Monkey Ball

97/100, gamecube

Almost flawless. The concept is so simple; balance a monkey in a ball across a stage to the goal post. It's surprisingly fun, and also incredibly challenging. I think the number of casual players that ever unlocked the master levels is very low (and sadly doesn't include me, but my friend managed to!)

Banjo Kazooie

97/100, n64

In my opinion, this was the platformer highlight of the N64, not SM64. It's charming, funny, quirky, and possibly has the coolest overworld I know. You need to collect quite a significant percentage of the available collectibles to progress, which makes the game the right amount of challenging. And have I mentioned Grant Kirkhope's perfect soundtrack???

Lylat Wars

95/100, n64

Watched in awe while my parents played this when I was very young, and got into it myself when it came out on the Virtual Console of the Wii. I could not put it down. I've done endless attempts to improve my high score and collect all the medals. Many of the quotes still live rent-free in my head. Do a barrel roll!

Hades

95/100, pc

Did not know rogue-like could be this fun, and I'm still impressed by the seemingly never-ending amount of unique dialogue.

Super Monkey Ball 2

94/100, gamecube

The sequel is more accessible than the original, which made it even more fun for me. The stage design is not as simple and elegant as in the game, but that did not bother me.

What do the tiers mean?

The tier a game belongs to is how 'impactful' it was to me, how much I enjoyed it given its time and context. There might be several cases where a high tier does not automatically imply a recommendation.

What about the xx/100 ratings?

These indicate how high I rate the quality of the game (also given its time period). They're supposed to be a bit more objective than the tiers, so a high rating should generally mean I recommend the game. There's a strong correlation between high tiers and high ratings though.

Notable exceptions are for example games that mean a lot to me, but have a low rating because they're bad games. Vice versa, some high quality games didn't click for me, so they ended up in a low tier.
